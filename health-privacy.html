Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy

Effective: April 29, 2026 Last updated: April 29, 2026

What this Policy is

This Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy describes how Pace handles consumer health data under the Washington My Health My Data Act (RCW 19.373).

This Policy supplements the general Pace Privacy Policy — it does not replace it. Capitalized terms not defined here have the meaning given in the general Privacy Policy. The general Privacy Policy contains additional information about Pace's overall privacy practices, security, beta program operation, and how this Policy is updated.

What is "consumer health data"?

Under Washington law, consumer health data is personal information that is linked or reasonably linkable to you and that identifies your past, present, or future physical or mental health status. This includes things like body measurements, vital signs, fitness data, dietary information, sleep records, biometric data, and any data we use to identify your health status.

1. Categories of consumer health data we collect, and purposes for collection

1.1 Information you provide directly

  • Body measurements: weight, height, age, biological sex
  • Activity level and fitness goals: self-reported activity classification, goals such as fat loss, muscle gain, or maintenance
  • Dietary information: food log entries (food name, portion, time of meal), photos of food, photographs of nutrition labels, scanned barcodes
  • Coach conversation content: any health-related information you share with the Pace coach in chat
  • Exercise records: workouts you log through the coach (exercise name, sets, reps, weight, date)

1.2 Information from Apple HealthKit (only with your authorization)

When you authorize Pace to read HealthKit data through iOS, we may receive any of the following categories you've authorized:

  • Workouts (workout type, duration, calories, heart rate during workout)
  • Activity (steps, active calories, exercise minutes)
  • Heart rate and heart rate variability (HRV)
  • Sleep records (sleep duration, sleep stages where available)
  • Body measurements (weight, body fat percentage, lean body mass)
  • Resting heart rate

You control which HealthKit data types are shared with Pace through iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.

1.3 Purposes

We collect and use consumer health data to:

  • Provide the Pace Services, including displaying your nutrition, activity, and recovery information; generating personalized coach guidance; identifying food items in photos; tracking progress over time
  • Improve the Services through de-identified, aggregated analysis
  • Communicate with you regarding your account
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not use consumer health data for advertising, marketing, profiling for inferences unrelated to the Services, or any purpose not listed above.

2. Sources of consumer health data

We collect consumer health data from the following sources:

  • Directly from you, when you create your account, complete onboarding, log food, take photos, chat with the coach, or interact with the Services
  • From your iOS device, specifically through Apple HealthKit, with your explicit authorization granted through iOS system permissions

We do not purchase consumer health data from data brokers, receive consumer health data from advertising networks, or obtain consumer health data from any source other than you and your authorized devices.

3. Categories of consumer health data we share

We share the categories of consumer health data described in Section 1 with the third parties identified in Section 4, for the purposes identified in Section 4. We share the minimum necessary for each provider's specific function.

We do not sell consumer health data. RCW 19.373.030 and 19.373.070 require separate written authorization before any sale of consumer health data.

We do not share consumer health data with advertising networks or for advertising or marketing purposes.

We do not share consumer health data for cross-context behavioral advertising.

5. Your Washington consumer health data rights

Under RCW 19.373.040, Washington State residents have the following rights with respect to their consumer health data.

5.1 Right to confirm and access

You have the right to confirm whether Pace is collecting, sharing, or selling your consumer health data, and to access that data. Upon a verified request, Pace will also provide, to the extent required by RCW 19.373.040(1)(a), a list of all third parties and affiliates with whom Pace has shared or sold your consumer health data and an active email address or other online mechanism that you may use to contact those recipients.

5.2 Right to deletion

You have the right to request deletion of your consumer health data. When you exercise this right, Pace will:

  • Delete your consumer health data from our active records and systems
  • Notify all affiliates, processors, contractors, and other third parties with whom Pace has shared your consumer health data of your deletion request, as required by RCW 19.373.040(1)(c)
  • Delete or overwrite archived and backup copies as soon as reasonably practicable within our backup lifecycle, in any case not exceeding the period permitted by applicable law (which is up to six months for archive and backup systems under Washington law)

You may delete your account at any time through Pace's in-app Settings, which initiates the deletion process described above. You may also request deletion by emailing privacy@usepace.fit.

We may decline a deletion request only as permitted by RCW 19.373.040(2). If we decline, we will explain why and inform you of your right to appeal.

5.3 Right to withdraw consent

You have the right to withdraw your consent for Pace to collect and share your consumer health data at any time. Withdrawing consent will limit the functionality of the Services because Pace requires consumer health data to provide its core features.

You can withdraw consent by:

  • Revoking specific HealthKit permissions in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health
  • Adjusting consent toggles in Pace's Settings
  • Deleting your account
  • Emailing privacy@usepace.fit

5.4 How to exercise your rights

To exercise any of the rights above, you may either:

  • Submit the request through the in-app account or privacy settings if that option is available to you, or
  • Email privacy@usepace.fit with a description of your request

Pace will not require you to create a new account in order to exercise these rights but may require you to authenticate through an existing account where appropriate.

We will:

  • Acknowledge receipt within 5 business days
  • Verify your identity (typically by confirming you can respond from the email address associated with your Pace account)
  • Respond substantively within 45 days, or notify you of an extension if more time is needed for complex requests

There is no fee to exercise these rights. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

5.5 How to appeal a decision

If we deny your request, you have the right to appeal. To submit an appeal:

  • Email privacy@usepace.fit with the subject line "Appeal"
  • Include a copy of or reference to our original decision
  • Explain why you believe the decision should be reconsidered

We will respond to your appeal within 45 days. If we deny your appeal, we will provide you with an online mechanism or other method through which you may contact the Washington Attorney General to submit a complaint. The Washington Attorney General's Office accepts complaints at www.atg.wa.gov/file-complaint.

6. Updates to this Policy

We may update this Policy. For material changes affecting how consumer health data is collected, used, or shared, we will obtain your affirmative consent before applying the change to data we already hold, as required by RCW 19.373.030(1)(c)–(d).
