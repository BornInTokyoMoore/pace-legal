A note from the team

Hi — I'm Chris, the founder of Pace. Pace is a small, independent app I built to help you bring your health data together — what you eat, how you train, how you recover, and how it all connects.

Because Pace works with personal health information, I take privacy seriously. This Policy describes what data Pace collects, how it's used, who I share it with, and the rights you have over your data. I've tried to write it in plain English, with the legal precision needed where the law requires it.

If you're a Washington State resident or you connect health-related data to Pace, please also read our Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy, which provides additional disclosures required by the Washington My Health My Data Act.

If anything in this Policy is unclear, email me at privacy@usepace.fit.

2. About this Policy

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how Born in Tokyo Digital LLC ("Pace," "we," "our," or "us") collects, uses, shares, and protects your personal information when you use the Pace iOS app, the usepace.fit website, and related services (together, the "Services").

This Policy applies to all users of Pace, including users currently participating in the Pace TestFlight closed beta program.

3. What information we collect

Pace collects the following categories of information.

3.1 Information you provide directly

  • Account information. When you create a Pace account using Sign in with Apple, we receive your name and email address as provided by Apple. You may also set a display name within the app.
  • Profile information. During onboarding, you may provide your age, weight, height, biological sex, activity level, dietary preferences, and fitness goals.
  • Food log entries. When you log food, we collect the food item, portion, time, and meal classification (breakfast, lunch, etc.).
  • Food photos. When you use the camera to log food, we receive the photo for the limited purpose of analyzing it for nutrition information.
  • Barcode and nutrition label photos. When you scan a barcode or photograph a nutrition label, we receive the image for analysis.
  • Coach chat messages. When you chat with the Pace coach, we receive your messages.
  • Exercise logs. When you log a workout through the coach, we receive the exercise name, sets, reps, and weight.
  • Body weight entries. When you log weight, we receive the value and the date.
  • Feedback and support requests. When you contact us, we receive what you send.

3.2 Information we collect from your device

  • Apple HealthKit data (only with your explicit authorization through iOS). When you authorize Pace to read HealthKit data, we receive: workout records, step counts, heart rate, heart rate variability, sleep records, body weight, and other health metrics you've authorized. You control which data types are shared through iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health. You can revoke access at any time.
  • Device information. Device model, iOS version, language, and time zone.
  • Diagnostic information. Crash reports and performance data automatically collected through the Sentry SDK to help us identify and fix technical issues. We have configured Sentry to scrub personally identifiable information from crash reports before transmission.
  • Usage information. De-identified interaction events automatically collected through the PostHog SDK to help us understand how Pace is used. Events do not include the content of your food logs, photos, or coach messages.

3.3 Information we receive from Apple

  • Authentication tokens through Sign in with Apple, used to log you into Pace.
  • Subscription status (post-launch) through Apple's App Store, indicating whether your subscription is active. We do not receive your payment card information.

3.4 Information we do NOT collect

For clarity: Pace does not collect, and has no access to, the contents of your iOS Photos library other than photos you specifically select and share with Pace. We do not access your contacts, your microphone, your camera roll, your location, your messages, your browsing history, or any other personal information beyond what's listed in Section 3.1, 3.2, and 3.3.

4. How we collect information

We collect information through:

  • Direct submission when you create an account, complete onboarding, log food, take photos, chat with the coach, or contact us.
  • iOS HealthKit with your explicit authorization, granted through iOS system permissions.
  • Automatic collection through SDKs integrated into the app (Sentry for diagnostics, PostHog for analytics, Apple's authentication services).
  • Third-party authentication through Sign in with Apple.

5. How we use your information

We use your information for the following purposes.

5.1 To provide the Services

  • Create and maintain your account
  • Display your nutrition, activity, and recovery data in the app
  • Enable the Pace coach to provide personalized guidance based on your data
  • Process food photos, barcodes, and nutrition labels through AI analysis to identify nutrition information
  • Sync data across your devices

5.2 To improve the Services

  • Diagnose technical issues using crash reports
  • Understand product usage patterns through de-identified analytics
  • Test and improve features

5.3 To communicate with you

  • Send transactional emails (account confirmations, password resets, billing receipts when applicable)
  • Respond to support requests
  • Notify you of material changes to this Policy or our Terms of Service

5.4 To comply with legal obligations

  • Respond to lawful requests from regulators or law enforcement
  • Defend against legal claims
  • Comply with Apple App Store Review Guidelines and applicable laws

5.5 What we do NOT do with your information

Pace does not sell your personal information or consumer health data, and does not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. We do not run advertisements in the Pace app. We do not provide your data to data brokers.

Limited analytics and diagnostic data flows to our service providers (Sentry, PostHog) may be considered "sharing" under California law unless treated as service-provider/contractor processing. We treat these flows as service-provider/contractor processing and contractually restrict these providers from using the data for any purpose other than providing services to Pace.

We do not use your personal information for any purpose other than those listed in Section 5.1 through 5.4.

7. How long we keep your information

We retain personal information by category, applying the following criteria:

  • Account profile and core health and wellness records (your account information, profile data, food logs, coach conversations, exercise logs, and HealthKit data Pace has stored): retained while your account remains active and for a limited period thereafter as reasonably necessary to complete deletion workflows, resolve disputes, maintain security records, or comply with legal obligations.
  • Crash and diagnostic logs (Sentry): retained for the shorter of the period needed for debugging and security or the vendor's standard log retention period, unless longer retention is legally required.
  • De-identified usage events (PostHog): retained on the vendor's standard analytics retention schedule and not linked to your personal identity.
  • Submitted photos not saved to meal history (food photos that you do not save as a logged meal): retained only for the time reasonably necessary to complete processing and confirm result delivery.
  • Backups and archives: retained on rolling schedules and deleted or overwritten in the ordinary backup cycle, subject to legal holds or security needs. For Washington consumer health data, backup deletion will not exceed the period permitted under RCW 19.373.040.
  • Billing and tax records (post-launch, when applicable): retained as required by applicable tax and accounting law.
  • De-identified, aggregated data that cannot reasonably be linked back to you may be retained indefinitely.

When you delete your account, your data is removed from our active systems immediately, your Sign in with Apple token is revoked, and backups are deleted in the ordinary backup cycle.

8. How we keep your information secure

We use technical and organizational safeguards to protect your information, including:

  • Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS for all communication between the app and our servers, between our servers and third parties).
  • Encryption at rest (Supabase encrypts stored data).
  • Access controls (only authorized personnel can access user data, and only for legitimate operational purposes).
  • Authentication (Sign in with Apple uses Apple's secure authentication framework).

No security measures are perfect. While we work hard to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. If we become aware of a security incident affecting your information, we will notify you in accordance with applicable law.

9. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have specific rights over your personal information. Pace honors these rights for all users, regardless of jurisdiction, except where otherwise indicated.

9.1 Rights available to all users

  • Access. You can view your data within the Pace app or request a copy by emailing privacy@usepace.fit.
  • Correction. You can edit your profile information within the app, or request corrections by email.
  • Deletion. You can delete your account from within Pace's Settings. Account deletion removes your data from our active systems immediately and from backups in our ordinary backup cycle.
  • Revoke HealthKit access. You can revoke Pace's access to HealthKit data at any time through iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.
  • Withdraw consent. Where we rely on consent, you can withdraw it at any time.
  • Data portability. You can request a copy of your data in machine-readable format by emailing privacy@usepace.fit.
  • Lodge a complaint. You can file a complaint with your local data protection authority.

9.2 California residents (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents have additional rights:

  • Right to know what personal information we have collected about you, including categories, sources, purposes, and recipients.
  • Right to delete your personal information.
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt-out of sale or sharing. Pace does not sell your personal information and does not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising. There is nothing to opt out of, but you have the right.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information. You can ask us to limit how we use sensitive personal information (such as health and fitness data) to what's necessary to provide the Services.
  • Right to non-discrimination. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

To exercise California rights, email privacy@usepace.fit. We will verify your identity (typically by confirming you can access the email associated with your account) and respond within 45 days.

9.3 Washington State residents

If you are a Washington State resident, please also see our Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy, which describes additional rights under the Washington My Health My Data Act.

10. Children's privacy

During the Pace TestFlight beta, the Services are intended only for users who are at least 18 years old. We do not knowingly permit anyone under 18 to create an account or use the Services during the beta. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a person under 18 in connection with the beta, we will delete that information as required by applicable law.

If Pace later opens the Services to younger users (after the public App Store launch), we will update this Policy and implement age-appropriate consent controls — including parental consent where required by law — before doing so.

11. International users

Pace is offered only to users located in the United States during the TestFlight beta. We do not intend to offer the beta to individuals located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, Brazil, Canada, or other jurisdictions outside the United States.

If you are located outside the United States, do not download, register for, or use Pace during the beta. We may refuse or terminate accounts we identify as created from outside the United States, but we cannot guarantee perfect detection of every user's location, and your responsibility to comply with this restriction does not depend on our ability to detect your location. By using the Services, you represent that you are physically located in the United States.

International availability and country-specific compliance will be addressed before Pace's public App Store launch.

13. How we update this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. When we make material changes (changes to what we collect, how we use it, or who we share it with), we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this Policy.
  • Increment the version number.
  • Notify you within the app and/or by email before the change takes effect.
  • For material changes affecting consumer health data, obtain your affirmative consent before applying the change to data we already hold.

For non-material changes (typo fixes, contact information updates, clarifying language), we will update the "Last Updated" date and the minor version number. No notification is required for non-material changes.

Previous versions of this Policy are available upon request to privacy@usepace.fit.
